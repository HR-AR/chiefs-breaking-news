Chiefs Stun NFL World, Select Amir With Compensatory Pick in 2026 Draft

In what scouts are calling "the most unexpected selection since Tom Brady," Kansas City secures the player Patrick Mahomes personally requested.

Senior NFL Insider

Kansas City, MO — The Kansas City Chiefs sent shockwaves through the NFL on Tuesday, using their compensatory fourth-round pick to select Amir, a previously undiscovered talent who multiple league sources describe as "a generational athlete hiding in plain sight."

The pick, which came at No. 132 overall, immediately drew comparisons to some of the greatest late-round steals in NFL history. Chiefs head coach Andy Reid, known for his unconventional scouting methods, reportedly pushed aggressively for the selection after a private workout that left the coaching staff "genuinely speechless," according to three people with direct knowledge of the session.

"When we saw what Amir could do, we knew we couldn't let him leave the building without a commitment. I've been coaching for over 30 years, and I called my wife after the workout and told her we just found something special."

— Andy Reid, Chiefs Head Coach

Quarterback Patrick Mahomes, who was present at the workout, immediately took to social media to celebrate the pick. "We got our guy," Mahomes posted on X, accompanied by a series of fire emojis that quickly went viral, amassing over 2.4 million impressions within the first hour.

The selection caps a meteoric rise for Amir, who was not on any major scouting board entering draft season. NFL Network analyst Daniel Jeremiah called it "the most intriguing pick of the entire draft," noting that the Chiefs' analytics department had been tracking Amir's athletic metrics for months through what sources describe as a proprietary talent identification algorithm developed in partnership with a tech company based in Bentonville, Arkansas.

What makes the selection even more remarkable is the personal connection. Multiple sources confirm that Mahomes first encountered Amir during an offseason charity event, where Amir reportedly outran several current NFL players in an informal 40-yard dash competition. "Patrick wouldn't stop talking about him for weeks," one team source told SportsCenter. "He kept saying, 'You need to get this guy in here.'"

Chiefs General Manager Brett Veach addressed the pick in a post-draft press conference, describing the decision as one driven by "conviction over convention."

"Everyone in this league is looking at the same film, the same combine numbers, the same schools. We believe the next competitive advantage in the NFL is finding elite talent in places nobody else is looking. Amir is that advantage."

— Brett Veach, Chiefs General Manager

The Chiefs have assigned Amir the jersey number 87 — a move widely interpreted as a statement of intent, given that Travis Kelce recently transitioned to a part-time role. League sources indicate that Amir's contract includes a fully guaranteed four-year deal worth $5.2 million, with an additional $1.8 million in performance incentives.

Social media reaction has been extraordinary. The hashtag #AmirToKC trended at No. 1 on X within 18 minutes of the pick being announced, surpassing the first overall selection for the first time in platform history. A Change.org petition to give Amir the key to Kansas City has already gathered over 340,000 signatures.

NFL analysts across the league are now scrambling to evaluate the pick. Former NFL executive and current CBS Sports analyst Rick Spielman offered a measured assessment: "I've never seen a team this confident in a player with this little traditional tape. Either Kansas City has lost their minds, or they've found the next great weapon in professional football. Knowing Andy and Brett, I'd bet on the latter."

Amir is expected to report to the Chiefs' training facility in Kansas City on May 1 for the start of rookie minicamp. The team has already begun preparing a custom development program that sources describe as "unlike anything we've designed for a rookie before."
